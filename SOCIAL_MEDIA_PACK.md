- **Icon area:** Center 50% of the image

- Font: Inter Bold or custom geometric letterforms
- Letter spacing: Tight (-0.02em)
- Vertical alignment: Optically centered
- Style: Clean, minimal, geometric

## 4. LinkedIn Post Templates (10 Topics)

### Template Structure

Each post follows this format:
- **Hook:** Opening line (stops the scroll)
- **Body:** 3-5 key points or narrative
- **Insight:** The takeaway
- **CTA:** Soft call to action
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant tags

---

### Post 1: Cloud Decision Framework

**Hook:**
"Cloud vs. on-premise" is the wrong question.

**Body:**
The right question is: "What constraints actually matter for THIS workload?"

Consider:
→ Data sovereignty requirements
→ Integration dependencies
→ Operational capability
→ Total cost over 5 years
→ Exit strategy complexity

**Insight:**
The best architecture is often hybrid. But "hybrid" means different things to different organizations.

**CTA:**
What's driving your infrastructure decisions right now?

**Hashtags:**
#CloudStrategy #ITDecisions #DigitalTransformation #TechLeadership

---

### Post 2: Vendor Selection Reality

**Hook:**
Vendor demos are designed to impress. Your evaluation process should be designed to reveal.

**Body:**
What demos show: Best-case scenarios, polished workflows, ideal configurations.

What you need to know:
→ How it handles YOUR edge cases
→ Integration with YOUR existing systems
→ Support response for YOUR region
→ Total cost including customization
→ Exit complexity if it doesn't work

**Insight:**
The best vendor for your competitor may not be the best for you. Context matters more than features.

**CTA:**
How do you structure your vendor evaluations?

**Hashtags:**
#VendorSelection #ITStrategy #B2BDecisions #TechProcurement

---

### Post 3: Compliance Readiness

**Hook:**
Compliance is not a project with an end date. It's an operating capability.

**Body:**
Organizations that struggle with compliance often treat it as:
✗ A one-time checkbox exercise
✗ An IT-only responsibility
✗ Something to address when auditors ask

Organizations that succeed treat it as:
✓ Continuous monitoring and improvement
✓ Cross-functional accountability
✓ Built into processes, not bolted on

**Insight:**
The gap between "compliant" and "audit-ready" is often larger than expected.

**CTA:**
Is your compliance posture sustainable or just current?

**Hashtags:**
#Compliance #PDPL #Cybersecurity #RiskManagement #GCC

---

### Post 4: Risk Framing

**Hook:**
"What's the risk?" is too vague. Better question: "Risk of what, to whom, over what timeframe?"

**Body:**
A proper risk assessment considers:

WHAT: Technical failure, data breach, vendor lock-in, cost overrun
WHO: Operations, customers, regulators, shareholders
WHEN: Immediate, 1-year, 5-year horizon
HOW LIKELY: Based on evidence, not fear
HOW SEVERE: Quantified where possible

**Insight:**
Vague risk discussions lead to analysis paralysis. Structured risk framing enables decisions.

**CTA:**
How do you frame technology risks for leadership?

**Hashtags:**
#RiskManagement #ITGovernance #TechLeadership #DecisionMaking

---

### Post 5: Scale vs. Stability

**Hook:**
"We need to scale" and "we need stability" are both valid. The question is: in what order, for which components?

**Body:**
Not everything needs to scale the same way:

→ Customer-facing apps: Scale fast, iterate frequently
→ Core transactions: Stability first, scale carefully
→ Analytics: Scale compute, not complexity
→ Integration: Design for stability, plan for scale

**Insight:**
Blanket "scale everything" approaches often create the instability they're meant to prevent.

**CTA:**
Where are you prioritizing scale vs. stability?

**Hashtags:**
#TechStrategy #Scalability #ITArchitecture #DigitalTransformation

---

### Post 6: Governance That Works

**Hook:**
IT governance fails when it's about control. It succeeds when it's about clarity.

**Body:**
Signs of governance problems:
✗ Decisions take months
✗ Nobody knows who approves what
✗ Shadow IT everywhere
✗ Post-mortems blame "process"

Signs of governance working:
✓ Clear decision rights
✓ Appropriate escalation paths
✓ Fast "no" is better than slow "maybe"
✓ Regular review and improvement

**Insight:**
Good governance makes decisions faster, not slower.

**CTA:**
Does your governance enable or obstruct?

**Hashtags:**
#ITGovernance #TechLeadership #DigitalTransformation #Leadership

---

### Post 7: The Cost of Waiting

**Hook:**
"Let's wait for more information" is sometimes wisdom. Sometimes it's just expensive delay.

**Body:**
Questions to distinguish:

→ What specific information are we waiting for?
→ When will we have it?
→ What's the cost of the delay?
→ Will the information actually change our decision?
→ Are we waiting or avoiding?

**Insight:**
Perfect information never arrives. The goal is sufficient information for a reversible or acceptable-risk decision.

**CTA:**
What technology decision has been "pending" too long?

**Hashtags:**
#DecisionMaking #TechLeadership #ITStrategy #Leadership

---

### Post 8: Multi-Cloud Reality

**Hook:**
Multi-cloud by strategy is different from multi-cloud by accident.

**Body:**
Multi-cloud by accident:
→ Different teams chose different providers
→ No consistent security posture
→ No cost visibility across platforms
→ No skill development strategy

Multi-cloud by strategy:
→ Clear workload placement criteria
→ Unified governance and security
→ Intentional redundancy decisions
→ Managed complexity

**Insight:**
"We use AWS and Azure" isn't a strategy. It's a description.

**CTA:**
Is your multi-cloud intentional or inherited?

**Hashtags:**
#MultiCloud #CloudStrategy #AWS #Azure #ITStrategy

---

### Post 9: When to Standardize

**Hook:**
Standardization reduces complexity. Except when it creates different complexity.

**Body:**
Good candidates for standardization:
✓ Security controls
✓ Integration patterns
✓ Development practices
✓ Monitoring approaches

Poor candidates for standardization:
✗ One-size-fits-all tools
✗ Processes that ignore context
✗ "Best practices" from different industries
✗ Solutions looking for problems

**Insight:**
Standardize principles. Be flexible on implementation.

**CTA:**
Where has standardization helped—or hurt—your organization?

**Hashtags:**
#ITStrategy #Standardization #TechLeadership #EnterpriseArchitecture

---

### Post 10: Advisory vs. Implementation

**Hook:**
Sometimes you need someone to tell you what to do. Sometimes you need someone to do it. They're different skills.

**Body:**
Advisory value:
→ Objective analysis without implementation bias
→ Fresh perspective on internal assumptions
→ Structured decision frameworks
→ Documented trade-offs

Implementation value:
→ Deep execution expertise
→ Hands-on problem solving
→ Accountability for delivery
→ Continuous optimization

**Insight:**
The conflict of interest appears when the same party advises AND implements. Separation creates clarity.

**CTA:**
How do you structure advisory vs. implementation relationships?

**Hashtags:**
#ITConsulting #TechAdvisory #ProjectManagement #Consulting

| Body | Inter | Regular (400) | 16px |

### Design Principles
1. **Clean negative space** — Let content breathe
2. **Minimal color palette** — Navy accent on neutral base
3. **Strong typography hierarchy** — Size and weight differentiate
4. **No gradients or effects** — Flat, professional design
5. **Consistent alignment** — Grid-based layouts

## 8. Usage Checklist

Before publishing any social asset:

- [ ] Correct dimensions for platform
- [ ] Text within safe zones
- [ ] Colors match brand palette
- [ ] Typography is Inter/Tajawal only
- [ ] No superlatives or guarantees in copy
- [ ] Professional tone maintained
- [ ] Arabic version reviewed for RTL
- [ ] Logo has proper clear space
- [ ] File optimized for web (compressed)
- [ ] Consistent with other brand assets
